## GotoKnow.org

**Verdict.** Heterogeneous platform. Strong individual authors (Vicharn
Panich) are bona-fide Model-tier for plain-diary personal-essay register;
others are pedagogy-listicle or literary-memoir at different registers.
Keep as Model **but author-scoped**, not blanket platform.

### Sample 1 — Vicharn Panich, ชีวิตที่พอเพียง series (post 727740)

URL: https://www.gotoknow.org/posts/727740

Features observed (no extended quoting):

- 1st-person `ผม` throughout. Plain-diary register, dated entry.
- Classifier-appropriate measurement (room number, distance, time —
  Thai numerals used consistently).
- Sentence rhythm: short-to-medium clauses, period-light, Thai-comma
  segmentation. Compounds joined with `และ` / `ตอน` / `ที่` rather
  than dashes or English-style listing commas.
- Closes with a soft reflective tail (`ซึ่งจะได้อีกบรรยากาศหนึ่ง`),
  not an AI-style aphorism kicker.

**Tier: Model (essay) — daily-diary register, plain literate Thai.**

### Sample 2 — Rapeephan Thilanan, pedagogy listicle (post 727736)

URL: https://www.gotoknow.org/posts/727736

Features observed:

- Heading-driven listicle, parenthetical English term per item
  (`(Experiential Anchoring)`, `(Mind Maping & Concept Map)` etc.).
- Calque density: moderate. Bracketed English heads throughout.
- Sentence rhythm: not flowing prose; pedagogy-notes shape.

**Tier: not a personal-essay model. Contributor-doc / teacher-notes
adjacent; out of scope for the register we were vetting.**

### Sample 3 — นายกำจัด คงหนู, "My way 11" / วิถีแห่งข้า series (post 727737)

URL: https://www.gotoknow.org/posts/727737

Features observed:

- Literary-memoir register. `ข้าพเจ้า` pronoun (archaic 1st-person),
  high-archaic vocabulary across the piece.
- Calque density: effectively zero. Native idiom.
- Sentence rhythm: lyrical, long compound sentences, period-light,
  nominalized clause structures.

**Tier: Model (literary-essay / archaic-narrative) — distinct from
plain personal-blog. Flag that voice is archaic; do not lift its
register into casual/marketing exemplars.**

### Overall GotoKnow conclusion

`register.md` Models list: author-scope GotoKnow, not blanket platform.

- Vicharn Panich — Model for plain-diary personal-essay.
- Literary-memoir authors ("My way" series) — Model only for the
  literary-essay sub-register; voice is archaic.
- Pedagogy authors — explicitly NOT a personal-essay model.

### Evidence — flagship profile feature (live, May 2026)

URL: https://readthecloud.co/ปราง-ปรางทิพย์-อายุ/

Tells observed (paraphrased; not quoting at length):

  emphasis.
- Repeated emphatic adverbs (`อย่างสิ้นเชิง`, `อย่างเงียบๆ`) as a

piece reads like this, the standard is no longer model-grade.

**Tier: drop from Models. Do not cite the live site. If the original
Read The Cloud archive is recoverable, specific features can serve,

### Storylog Group successor platforms — Fictionlog / Tunwalai / Pinto

Per chakrit (2026-05-13), explored as candidate sources. **Verdict:
different register family — needs its own vetting pass, not added
ad-hoc to existing personal-essay slot.**

- Fictionlog — serial fiction (per-chapter sale model).
- Tunwalai — romance / popular fiction at scale.
- Pinto — e-book retail; not a content platform per se. Skip.

These are **popular-fiction / web-novel register** — distinct from any
of the current 5 register families. Authors are amateur-to-semi-pro;
not editorial prose. High volume of native-Thai writing, but with
heavy genre conventions (dialogue, tropes) that don't generalize to
non-fiction registers.

**Author-pool caveat (chakrit, 2026-05-13).** A large share of fiction
on these platforms is written by teenagers and other untrained writers
— no professional Thai-writing background, no editorial pass. Even
the *fiction* register would be unsafe to lift from blindly: amateur
conventions (clichéd dialogue, school-essay vocabulary, narrative-
trope mimicry) would contaminate any patterns extracted. Future
vetting must select for *experienced* / *paid-out* author profiles
(Fictionlog publicly lists per-author earnings) and skip the amateur
long tail. Same caveat applies to Minimore — same author profile.

Queued in `notes/research-queue.md` as a separate vetting target.

## Pantip long-form (Blueplanet)

**Verdict.** Strong source for adult personal-blog register. Identified
bylines with consistent voice across post series. Confirm as Model.

### Sample — settembre, "เล่าสู่กันฟัง ณ อิตาลี" series (post 44084956)

URL: https://pantip.com/topic/44084956

Author "settembre" / ปุ๊กกี้ — Thai woman living in the Italian Alps,
recurring travel-diary series.

Features observed (no extended quoting):

- 1st-person `ดิฉัน` consistently. Polite-feminine choice; chosen, not
  defaulted.
- Sentence rhythm: short, fragmented, Thai-comma flow. Period-light.
- Conversational tail particles applied naturally (`นะคะ`, `ค่ะ`,
  `เนี่ย`). Period-particle pairing.
- Spoken-marker forms in writing (`เค้า` for `เขา`, casual
  reduplication) — colloquial-written, not formal-written.
- Natural bilingual code-switch in outro line (English aphorism after
  Thai body) without awkwardness.
- Emoji integrated into prose rhythm rather than tacked on.
- Voice consistency across multiple posts in the room.
- No AI tells: no em-dash aphorism kickers, no emphatic-adverb
  pacing, no factual contradictions, no scaffolded section
  transitions.

**Tier: Model (personal-blog adult-amateur).** Bylines with multi-post
voice consistency are individually identifiable and lift-worthy
(settembre, tuk-tuk@korat, fatREVIEW, Are you for real ?, etc.).

**Author-pool note.** Pantip long-form rooms (Blueplanet for travel,
Klai Baan for home/lifestyle, Greenzone, etc.) skew adult-amateur:
mid-career professionals, expats, retirees writing for fellow
travelers. Unlike Fictionlog/Tunwalai's teen author pool, these
writers have life experience and write in Thai for adult Thai
readers — the register we actually want to model.

**Caveat.** Pantip also hosts forum short-form and Q&A. Restrict
source-lifting to long-form review/diary rooms and to bylined posters
with multi-post histories. Anonymous `สมาชิกหมายเลข …` one-off posts
should not be lifted blindly.

## Corpus-quality concern surfaced 2026-05-22 — AWS Thailand b2b-formal

While auditing `forbidden-phrases.md` against the corpus, `ปฏิเสธไม่ได้ว่า`
and `มีความสำคัญ` both surfaced native-looking hits inside AWS Thailand
marketing copy (`aws-thailand-agentcore.md` opens with
`ในยุคของ AI agents คงปฏิเสธไม่ได้ว่า...`). Marketing copy from large
vendors in 2024-2026 is a plausible AI-drafted-Thai vector — these entries
should not be trusted as gold-standard native for blocklist-calibration or
exemplar-lifting purposes.

Action items:

- Treat AWS Thailand (and similarly: any large multinational vendor's
  Thai marketing pages indexed in 2024+) as **register reference for
  surface conventions** (terminology, ทับศัพท์ choices, structure) but
  **not as a quality reference for natural Thai rhetoric**.
- Re-vet existing curated entries under
  `corpus/curated/marketing/b2b-formal/aws-thailand-*.md` against this
  concern. Some may need to be downgraded to `raw/` or removed.
- Prefer Thai-founded company copy (Bluebik, Wisesight, Krungsri, SCB
  in-house, banks' own long-form) as primary b2b-formal anchors over
  multinational localizations.
